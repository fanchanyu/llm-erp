CHANGE 1 — Overclaim word: "achieving" → "yielding"
================================================================================
Location: Section 1 — Introduction, Contribution point 4 (line 74)
Before: "A preliminary experimental evaluation using 30 realistic test queries,
         achieving 90% end-to-end accuracy."
After:  "A preliminary experimental evaluation using 30 realistic test queries,
         yielding 90% end-to-end accuracy."
Rationale: "achieving" implies successful accomplishment; "yielding" is a
neutral, factual descriptor of observed outcomes.

================================================================================
CHANGE 2 — Weak hedging: added "on the test queries"
================================================================================
Location: Section 5.3 — Discussion of Results (line 243)
Before: "Five of seven categories reached 100% accuracy."
After:  "Five of seven categories reached 100% accuracy on the test queries."
Rationale: With only 30 test queries (4-5 per category), 100% accuracy on a
tiny sample could be misinterpreted as a definitive claim about system
capability. Adding "on the test queries" anchors the claim to the specific
evaluation set.

================================================================================
CHANGE 3 — Overclaim word: "substantially" → "notably"
================================================================================
Location: Section 5.3 — Discussion of Results (line 244)
Before: "...substantially improved accuracy."
After:  "...notably improved accuracy."
Rationale: "substantially" is an unquantified magnitude claim about the
improvement from prompt engineering. The evaluation did not measure effect
size. "notably" is more descriptive and less evaluative.

================================================================================
CHANGE 4 — Knowledge overclaim: "demonstrates" → "suggests"
================================================================================
Location: Section 6.1 — Summary of Findings (line 259)
Before: "This work demonstrates that LLM-mediated natural language interaction
         for manufacturing ERP is technically feasible across six modules."
After:  "This work suggests that LLM-mediated natural language interaction
         for manufacturing ERP is technically feasible across six modules."
Rationale: With one preliminary 30-query evaluation by the developer, the
evidence is indicative rather than conclusive. "Suggests" correctly frames
the finding as preliminary evidence, not established fact.

================================================================================
CHANGE 5 — Superlative adjective: "The most significant" → "A notable"
================================================================================
Location: Section 6.2 — Design Implications (line 263)
Before: "The most significant design finding is the importance of explicit
         tool routing rules."
After:  "A notable design finding is the importance of explicit tool routing
         rules."
Rationale: "The most significant" is an absolute superlative that implicitly
ranks all design findings. "A notable" is appropriately modest for a single
preliminary evaluation.
