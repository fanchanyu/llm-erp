## PART 2: JOURNAL SUBMISSION GUIDELINES

### Journal of Management Studies (JMS)
**Publisher:** Wiley
**ISSN:** 0022-2380 (print), 1467-6486 (online)
**Website:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/14676486

#### Literature Review Requirements:

1. **Initial screening criteria** (desk reject basis):
   - Editors evaluate suitability upon first receipt
   - Papers judged too weak due to "lacking relevant literature" may be **rejected at the desk** (before peer review)
   - Other desk reject reasons: methodological flaws, limited theoretical contribution

2. **Theoretical grounding is essential:**
   - "The journal attaches no priority to either the subjects of study, or the methodological approach adopted, so long as these are theoretically grounded"
   - "Our ultimate criterion for a [paper] is that these are theoretically grounded"

3. **Manuscript preparation:**
   - Must be submitted via ScholarOne at https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/jmstudies
   - Title, abstract, keywords on first page
   - Blind peer review: authors must avoid self-identifying references
   - Reference format follows Wiley house style

4. **Review process:**
   - Initial editorial decision: ~1 week
   - Full peer review: typically 60-90 days
   - Single-anonymous peer review (reviewers know authors, but authors don't know reviewers)

5. **Article types:**
   - Research Articles
   - JMS Says (short 300-word proposal for debate pieces)
   - Special Issue papers

#### Key Takeaway for Literature Review in JMS:
A strong literature review is critical. The editors explicitly state that "lacking relevant literature" is a desk-reject criterion. The paper must demonstrate theoretical grounding through comprehensive engagement with existing literature. The review is not just descriptive but must position the paper's theoretical contribution.

### International Journal of Production Research (IJPR)
**Publisher:** Taylor & Francis
**ISSN:** 0020-7543 (print), 1366-588X (online)
**Website:** https://www.tandfonline.com/toc/tprs20/current

#### Literature Review Requirements:

1. **Mandatory elements for ALL Research Articles:**
   - ✅ An **exhaustive analysis of related Production Research publications**
   - A novel decision aid model for design or management of production systems
   - ✅ **Comparisons with the state of the art**
   - Discussion on real life applications
   - Managerial insights for decision makers in industry
   - Research perspectives

2. **Mandatory elements for Review Articles:**
   - ✅ An **exhaustive analysis of related Production Research publications**
   - Analysis of novel decision aid models
   - ✅ **Comparisons of the state of the art approaches**
   - Discussion on real life applications
   - Managerial insights
   - Research perspectives

3. **Word limits:**
   - Research Articles: max 12,000 words (inclusive of abstract, tables, references, captions)
   - Review Articles: max 18,000 words
   - Discussion/Technical Notes: 2,500–4,000 words

4. **Abstract:** Unstructured, max 200 words

5. **References:** Any consistent scholarly format; DOI recommended; Chicago Author-Date style applied post-acceptance

6. **Peer review:** Single-anonymous (2 independent expert reviewers)

7. **Format-free submission:** No strict formatting requirements initially

#### Key Takeaway for Literature Review in IJPR:
IJPR explicitly requires an **"exhaustive analysis of related Production Research publications"** and **"comparisons with the state of the art"** as mandatory elements. These are non-negotiable — papers lacking this will be unsubmitted. The literature review must demonstrate comprehensive coverage of production research publications specifically.
